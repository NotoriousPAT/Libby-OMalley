Energetic. Creative. Resourceful. Seasoned non-profit professional with entrepreneurial flair. I feel it in my fingers. I feel it in my toes.

Libby O'Malley is the person that you want to work with and to play with. Currently, she's the part-time executive director for FredXchange, a non-profit organization that's fostering an entrepreneurial ecosystem in the Fredericksburg, VA region. She's also the founder and co-founder of two startups, daySmarts and Muster Me, respectively.

Libby was the executive director for the San Diego chapter of the American Institute of Architects for ten years and speaks and consults on non-profit organizations and association management based on her tenure at AIA San Diego and other non-profits like the Arthritis Foundation and Hampton EventMakers. A staunch patriot, Libby worked for the Navy for five years in the Morale, Welfare & Recreation Department, doing marketing, PR, event planning, and commercial sponsorship.

She has a BA from the University of Virginia and a Masters of Tourism Administration with a focus on special events from George Washington University. Some highlights of Libby's work experience are listed below.

As the Executive Director of FredXchange, Libby is an integral part of the organization's mission -- to launch an entrepreneurial ecosystem in the Fredericksburg, VA region by 2020. One of Libby's roles in this position is to launch and manage The Foundry, FredXchange's new coworking space. Connect with Libby through FredXchange to cover all things entrepreneurial and startup-focused.

Libby is the Founder and CEO of daySmarts, a startup organization founded in 2013. daySmarts is a personal calendar that features the dates and times of all the programs and events for your life's activities. Your sports team, children's schools, favorite museums, bands, and stores. The homeowners' association meetings. If it should be in your calendar, with daySmarts, it already IS in your calendar. daySmarts is a great example of Libby's ability to see a problem and devise a solution to address it. Check out daySmarts here.

The hassle of a group of individuals sharing their contact information with one another quickly on the spot prompted Libby and two partners to co-found to create and launch Muster Me. This resource allows people to text and share their info as a group -- there's not even an app required! Currently, Muster Me is being marketed to non-profits, power networkers, and reunion organizations.

Libby's newest venture, Patriyachtic, will set sail in mid-April. An online, lifestyle store focused on products and gifts that are "patriotically nautical," the site features everything from jewelry and clothing to artwork and food. Whether you're a Republican or a Democrat, a sailor or a power boater, Patriyachtic has the red, white, and blue boating items you seek!

Single. Adoptive. Moms. Making. It. Happen. On. Our Own. In 2009, Libby received a phone call that would change her life. Having waited for an adoption match from the San Diego County foster adoption process, Libby was thrilled to become a mommy to Morgan -- or, as everyone call her, "Bug." SAMMIHOOO is an online community of other single adoptive mommies -- for celebration, commiseration, and collaboration.
